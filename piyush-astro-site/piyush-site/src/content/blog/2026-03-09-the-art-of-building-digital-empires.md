Every great product starts with a single line of code written with intent.

## The Mindset of a Digital Architect

Building software is not just about making things that work. It's about crafting systems that *endure* — that scale gracefully under pressure, that feel effortless to use, and that stand the test of time.

Over a decade of building products across industries, I've come to believe that the best engineers think like architects. They don't just solve the problem in front of them — they design the foundation that makes the next ten problems trivial.

## Principles I Build By

**1. Simplicity is the ultimate sophistication.** Every abstraction you add is debt. Add it only when it pays for itself ten times over.

**2. The user doesn't care about your stack.** They care about how fast the page loads, how intuitive the interaction feels, and whether the product does what they came to do. Start there.

**3. Readable code is a gift to your future self.** You will read a piece of code 10 times more than you write it. Write it like you're leaving instructions for someone who just joined the team.

> "The details are not the details. They make the design." — Charles Eames
